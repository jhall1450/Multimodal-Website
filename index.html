Why is security important?

User accounts are an effective way of proving the identity of a user trying to connect to a website. The trouble with account security is that if one does not keep their account secure other people, some of whom may have malicous intent, can impersonate that person online. In the Services Associated with UC Accounts section, there is a non-exhaustive list of how many important places just your UC account is associated with. Imagine the danger associated with losing control of your UC account. If a user account is compromised, there is now a weak "trusted" point of access into the system/network. For example, compromised UC email accounts can be used to 'Phish' other users into providing personal and private details about themselves. Another user may trust a phishing email because it is coming from another student, staff, or faculty member at the University of Cincinnati.

Keeping your account secure not only protects yourself, but also other users at the University of Cincinnati.
